Biparite matching for job distribution in Ocaml

For my functional programming class I had to work on a small project in Ocaml. The subject was the Max Flow problem in directed graphs. With my project partner, we decided to work on bipartite matching and try to solve the Min Cost - Max Flow problem.

Here is the problem : we have n people that want to work. These applicants can have more than one job at a time. On the other hand we have k jobs and each accepts a maximum number of applicants. Each applicant will rank the jobs they want (they only choose the ones the want, they do not need to rank all the jobs). The most wanted job has a priority of 0, the one after 1 etc. And that's it ! The algorithm will try to maximize the Flow of the created graph to ensure that a maximum of jobs are taken but will minimize the cost to ensure that the applicants have the jobs they want the most. Here is the result on the right.

On the left we have applicants and on the right we have the jobs. The format on each edge is (x/1):y. If x = 1, the person has taken the job. y represents the priority that the applicant assigned to that job. For the source code and more information about the algorithm, you can go here (in french).
